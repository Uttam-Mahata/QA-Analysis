World War 3 could be triggered by a volatile mix of geopolitical tensions, economic rivalries, and technological competitions..
Rising nationalism, territorial disputes, and resource scarcity may escalate conflicts among major powers..
Economic inequalities and trade wars could exacerbate hostilities, while the race for technological dominance, especially in artificial intelligence and cyber warfare, may create new battlegrounds..
The proliferation of nuclear weapons, the potential for non-state actors to acquire them, and the impacts of climate change and mass migration further contribute to the risk..
The fragility of international institutions, reliance on critical infrastructure vulnerable to cyber attacks, and the potential for misinformation to manipulate public opinion underscore the need for robust diplomacy, international cooperation, and conflict prevention to avert a catastrophic global conflict.
