Social stratification is a sociological concept that refers to the hierarchical arrangement of individuals or groups within a society based on various attributes such as wealth, power, social class, education, and social status..
Social stratification creates unequal distribution of resources, opportunities, and privileges..
Social stratification influences power structures within societies..
Those in higher strata typically wield more influence in decision-making processes, shaping policies and institutions to serve their interests..
Social stratification results in the creation of social classes, each characterized by specific norms, values, and lifestyles..
Social stratification intersects with other forms of inequality, such as gender, race, and ethnicity..
Individuals facing multiple layers of stratification due to these factors may experience compounded disadvantages, deepening overall societal inequalities.Social classes often have distinct cultural norms and values..
This can lead to cultural and social divisions, limiting interactions and understanding between individuals from different strata.
