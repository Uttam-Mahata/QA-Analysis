Social stratification refers to the organization of people or groups in a society according to factors like wealth, power, education, and social standing..
This hierarchical structure often results in an uneven distribution of resources, opportunities, and privileges, significantly impacting individuals' life prospects..
Access to education, healthcare, and employment is influenced by one's position in the social hierarchy..
The perpetuation of inequality and the challenges of social mobility are inherent in stratified societies, potentially leading to discontent among those at the bottom..
Addressing social stratification is essential for mitigating disparities, fostering social unity, and building a more just and equitable society.
