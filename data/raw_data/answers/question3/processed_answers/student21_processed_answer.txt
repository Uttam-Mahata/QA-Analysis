Social stratification, the hierarchical organization of individuals or groups in a society based on factors such as wealth, power, education, and social status, shapes the distribution of resources, opportunities, and privileges..
This structured system significantly influences individuals' life chances, impacting their access to education, healthcare, and employment..
The perpetuation of inequality and the challenges of social mobility are inherent in stratified societies, potentially leading to discontent among those at the bottom..
Addressing social stratification is essential for mitigating disparities, fostering social unity, and building a more just and equitable society.
