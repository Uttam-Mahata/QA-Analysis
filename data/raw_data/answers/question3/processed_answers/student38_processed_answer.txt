Explain the concept of social stratification and its impact on societies..
Ans-
Social stratification refers to the categorization of people in a society into different groups based on factors such as wealth, income, education, occupation, and social status..
It is a hierarchical arrangement of individuals or groups based on various power and social status..
Social stratification has both positive and negative effects on societies..
It can provide individuals with a sense of identity and belonging within their social group and serve as a motivator for individuals to work hard and achieve success..
However, it can also lead to inequality, poverty, and social conflict..
Those in lower positions within the social hierarchy often face significant barriers to social mobility, which can lead to social unrest and conflict, particularly when individuals feel that the system is unjust or unfair..
Social stratification is prevalent in most societies, whether modern or traditional, and is a fundamental aspect of human societies.
