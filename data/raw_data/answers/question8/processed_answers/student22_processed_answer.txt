The Industrial Revolution, spanning the late 18th to the 19th centuries, fundamentally transformed the modern world.
Economically, it marked a shift from agrarian to industrialized and capitalist systems, propelling mass production and economic growth through the establishment of factories.
Technological innovations, such as steam engines and mechanized production, revolutionized manufacturing and transportation, reshaping societal structures with urbanization and the emergence of labor movements.
Globally, it spurred globalization, fostering expanded trade and communication networks.
Despite positive outcomes like improved living conditions and healthcare, the era also brought environmental challenges.
Education underwent a shift towards technical training, and culturally, the Industrial Revolution influenced literature, art, and societal values.
Its enduring legacy is evident in contemporary global economies, societal structures, and technological landscapes, underscoring its pivotal role in shaping the trajectory of human progress.
