The Industrial Revolution, spanning the late 18th to the 19th centuries, wrought profound changes on the modern world..
Marked by a shift from agrarian societies to industrialized and capitalist systems, this era introduced mass production and the establishment of factories, driving unprecedented economic growth..
Technological innovations, like steam engines and mechanized production, revolutionized manufacturing and transportation, reshaping societal structures with urbanization and the rise of industrial working classes..
Labor movements emerged in response to poor working conditions, advocating for workers' rights..
Globally, the revolution spurred globalization through expanded trade networks and improved communication..
While improving living conditions and healthcare, the era also brought environmental challenges..
Today, its legacy endures in the foundations of contemporary global economies, societal structures, and technological landscapes, leaving an indelible mark on human progress.
