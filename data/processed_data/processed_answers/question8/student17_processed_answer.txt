The Industrial Revolution, a transformative period from the late 18th to the 19th centuries, exerted a sweeping influence on the modern world.
Economically, it marked a shift from agrarian societies to industrialized, capitalist models, fueled by mass production and the establishment of factories.
Technological innovations, notably steam engines and mechanized production, revolutionized manufacturing and transportation, introducing advancements like steam locomotives and automobiles.
Urbanization surged as people migrated to burgeoning industrial centers, sparking social changes and labor movements advocating for workers' rights.
The global impact was profound, fostering globalization through increased trade and communication.
Despite positive outcomes such as improved living conditions and healthcare, environmental consequences also emerged.
The legacy of the Industrial Revolution endures, shaping contemporary global economies, societal structures, and technological landscapes.
